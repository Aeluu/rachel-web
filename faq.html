Frequently asked questions

How much experience do you have?

I have been working in product for more than 6 years, with the past 3 years focused on leading and running small product teams. Additionally, I have 2 years of art direction and advertising experience, with a focus on branding and marketing campaigns.

What kind of products have you worked on?

I have worked with a variety of products, but my experience is mostly related to B2C products. Many of the platforms were related to fintech and the majority of my experience is in a tech and start-up environment. I have worked on native iOS / Android applications, responsive web platforms, B2B SaaS applications and have even shipped features for the Apple Watch.

What kind of features have you worked on?

I am pretty familiar with payment structures, onboarding, and registration processes for native and web applications as that covers a big portion of what I actively worked on. Though, I believe all of these user experiences vary depending on the product. I'm really open to new challenges and solving other problems, and have a deep interest in working with deep-learning technology.

What are you good at?

I have excellent communication skills, in person, on paper or remotely! I communicate well internally within a company structure, and externally, to all users of any product. Being able to communicate well was what led me into a product management role. I also love doing presentations!

How big are the teams you lead?

My experience is mostly related to working with small companies (companies with about 20-40 people), so I'm more accustomed to leading teams that range from about 4-10 people. I wish I could say that I have the experience to lead in big team environments, but I haven't had the opportunity yet. However, I'm confident I can apply my current knowledge to those areas and really interested to take that challenge.

How would you describe your product roadmap process?

I create Product Roadmaps based on quarterly schedules that tend to be focused on company OKRs and KPIs. I prefer to work with companies that are familiar with releasing and hitting goals in an agile and sprint environment. My processes work well with a lot of tech companies, but sometimes they can change depending on the team size or project expectation.
