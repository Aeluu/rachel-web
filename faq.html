Frequently asked questions

How much experience do you have?

I have been working in product design for about 6 years, with the past 3 years focused on leading and running small teams. Additionally, I have 2 years of art direction and advertising experience, with a focus on branding and marketing campaigns.

What kind of products have you worked on?

I have worked with a variety of products, but my experience is mostly related to B2C products. I have worked on native iOS / Android applications, responsive web platforms, B2B SaaS applications and have even shipped features for the Apple Watch.

What is your strongest skill?

I have excellent communication skills, in person, on paper or remotely. I communicate well internally within a company structure, and externally, to all users of any product.

How big are the teams you lead?

My experience is mostly related to working with small companies (companies with about 30-60 people), so I'm more accustomed to leading teams that range from about 4-10 people. I've worked in corporate settings before (with +100 employees) and I am familiar with their processes, but never had the opportunity to lead a team in those large-scale environments. However, I'm confident I can apply my current knowledge to those areas.

How would you describe your product roadmap process?

I create Product Roadmaps based on quarterly schedules that tend to be focused on OKRs and KPIs. I prefer to work with companies that are familiar with releasing and hitting goals in an agile environment. My processes work well with a lot of tech companies, but sometimes they can change depending on the team size or project expectation. Here is an article I wrote about how I lead agile teams, and here is an article on how I incorporate design thinking processes with product teams.

What tools do you use?

For product management: Jira, Confluence and Trello. I'm a huge fan of Coda for documentation.

For product design: Sketch and Figma for design, InVision for fast prototyping, Principle and Framer for high-quality prototyping. I am familiar with Illustrator, Photoshop and Adobe XD, but I find other products far superior to Adobe, and would make teams switch if given the opportunity.

What other skills do you bring to the table?

I actually graduated with an Art Director degree in Advertising and worked with agencies for a few years. I am an excellent (English) copywriter, great at executing marketing campaigns, and have an exceptional skill at branding companies. I am also quite good at HTML / CSS and familiar with some Javascript.

Do you speak German?

I've been working on a lot of German products for the past few years, but unfortunately my German speaking skills are not good enough for a German-speaking environment, sorry! I am very familiar with the German market however.

How do you feel about remote work?

I have nothing against it! Considering the current COVID-19 scenario, I think it will be an opportunity for a lot of businesses in the near future. I have experience working and leading teams completely remotely. I personally prefer "hybrid" work environments, where half the work is on-site and the other half is off-site. You can ask me for further details regarding this!

How do you feel about relocating?

I am 100% open to relocating for the right opportunity! I've been in Berlin for the past 5 years and love it, but I'm very eager to design for other markets and other countries. I have lived and worked in San Francisco, Los Angeles, Seoul and Berlin.
